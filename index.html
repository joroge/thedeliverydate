Fertility Guide

Evidence-based fertility information. Sources: NICE CG156 · WHO Infertility · HFEA

⏱️ When to Seek Help

👩Under 35: Seek investigation after 12 months of regular unprotected sex. NHS ↗
👩Age 35–39: Seek investigation after 6 months.
👩Age 40+: Refer immediately — do not wait.
🗓️Fertile window: 5 days before ovulation + ovulation day. Sperm survives 5 days; egg 12–24 hours. NHS ↗
🌡️Ovulation signs: LH surge on OPK test, clear/stretchy cervical mucus ("egg white"), BBT rise 0.2°C after ovulation. ACOG ↗
🕐Sex frequency: Every 1–2 days during fertile window is optimal. Daily is not more effective.
⚠️Early referral: Irregular periods, PCOS, endometriosis history, previous STI, pelvic surgery, recurrent miscarriage. RCOG GTG 17 ↗
💊Pre-conception folic acid: 400mcg daily ≥1 month before conception. High-risk: 5mg prescribed. CDC ↗
📚 NICE CG156 Fertility Problems (2023 update)

🌿 Fertility Lifestyle Factors

⚖️BMI 18.5–24.9: Both underweight and overweight reduce fertility significantly. 5–10% weight change can restore ovulation. NHS ↗
🚭Stop smoking: Accelerates egg loss, damages sperm DNA, reduces IVF success by 50%. Most important lifestyle change. HFEA ↗
🍷Alcohol: Limit to under 4 units/week (women), under 14 units/week (men). Heavy drinking impairs fertility in both sexes. NHS ↗
🏃Exercise: Moderate 150 min/week is optimal. Excessive high-intensity exercise may suppress ovulation. ACOG ↗
😴Sleep 7–9 hours: Sleep deprivation disrupts FSH, LH, prolactin. Circadian rhythm supports ovulation. Sleep Foundation ↗
🧘Stress reduction: Chronic cortisol disrupts GnRH pulsatility. Mindfulness, yoga, acupuncture may help. RESOLVE ↗
🌡️Avoid heat (male): Saunas, hot baths, tight underwear, laptop on lap reduce sperm quality. Effects take 3 months to recover. WHO Semen Manual ↗

🔬 Female Fertility Tests

🧪
AMH — Anti-Müllerian Hormone
Ovarian reserve marker. Blood test on any day of cycle. Normal >1.0 ng/mL. Low AMH = reduced reserve. High may indicate PCOS. NICE ↗
📅
Day 2–3 FSH & LH
High FSH (>10 IU/L) = reduced reserve. LH:FSH ratio helps diagnose PCOS. Day 21 Progesterone confirms ovulation (>30 nmol/L). RCOG ↗
🔵
AFC — Antral Follicle Count (Ultrasound)
Counts 3–10mm follicles in both ovaries. AFC 7–16 = normal reserve. Best predictor of IVF egg yield. HFEA ↗
🔦
HyCoSy / HSG — Tubal Patency
Checks fallopian tube openness and uterine cavity. 20–30% of infertility is tubal factor. HyCoSy = ultrasound; HSG = X-ray. RCOG GTG 38 ↗
🧬
Karyotype & Genetic Screening
For recurrent miscarriage (>2 losses) or family history of genetic conditions. Carrier screening: sickle cell, cystic fibrosis, thalassaemia. RCOG GTG 17 ↗

🔬 Male Fertility Tests

🔬
Semen Analysis — WHO 2021 Reference Values
Volume ≥1.4ml · Concentration ≥16M/ml · Total motility ≥42% · Progressive motility ≥30% · Morphology ≥4% (Kruger strict). WHO Semen Manual 6th Ed ↗
🧬
Sperm DNA Fragmentation (SDF)
SDF >25% DFI correlates with recurrent miscarriage and poor IVF outcomes. Smoking, heat, oxidative stress increase SDF. ESHRE SDF ↗
🩸
Hormone Profile (FSH, LH, Testosterone)
Azoospermia + high FSH = testicular failure. Azoospermia + low FSH = obstructive/hormonal cause (treatable). NHS Infertility Diagnosis ↗
💊
Ovulation Induction (OI)
Letrozole (NICE first-line 2023) or Clomifene for anovulatory infertility. Monitored with serial USS. 3–6 cycles offered. NICE CG156 ↗
🔬
IUI — Intrauterine Insemination
Washed sperm placed at ovulation. 10–15% success/cycle. Used for unexplained infertility, mild male factor, donor sperm. HFEA ↗
⚗️
IVF & ICSI
~30% live birth per cycle under 35, declining with age. ICSI for severe male factor. HFEA IVF Data ↗
🧊
Egg/Embryo Freezing
Elective social freezing or medical preservation (cancer). Success depends on age at freezing — earlier is better. HFEA ↗

🥗 Male Fertility Nutrition — with Sources

🦪Zinc 11mg/day (oysters, beef, pumpkin seeds): Essential for sperm production and testosterone. NIH ODS ↗
🥜Selenium 100mcg/day (2 Brazil nuts): Antioxidant protecting sperm from oxidative damage. Improves motility. NIH ODS ↗
🍅Lycopene 4mg/day (cooked tomatoes, watermelon): Reduces sperm DNA fragmentation. 2 tbsp tomato sauce = 4mg.
🐟Omega-3 DHA (oily fish, walnuts): High-DHA diet associated with better sperm morphology and motility. PubMed Review ↗
🍊Vitamin C 1000mg: Antioxidant protecting sperm DNA. Reduces sperm agglutination. NIH ODS ↗
🌰CoQ10 200–600mg: Mitochondrial antioxidant. Significantly improves sperm motility and count in RCTs. PubMed RCT ↗
🌿Folic acid 400mcg + Zinc: Combined supplementation shown to improve sperm count in some studies. PubMed ↗
📚 WHO Semen Analysis Manual 6th Ed 2021 · NIH ODS

⚠️ What Harms Male Fertility — Evidence

🚭Smoking: Reduces count 13–17%, motility 10–17%, increases DNA fragmentation 40%. Takes 3 months to improve post-cessation. HFEA ↗
🍺Alcohol >14 units/week: Reduces testosterone, sperm count and quality. Even moderate drinking affects morphology. NHS ↗
🌡️Heat exposure: Testicles need 34°C (below body temp). Hot baths, saunas, tight underwear, laptop all harmful. Effects take 3 months to recover. WHO ↗
💉Anabolic steroids: Shut down the HPG axis → profound azoospermia. Can take 2+ years to recover. Tell GP immediately. NICE ↗
⚖️Obesity BMI >30: Reduces testosterone, raises oestrogen. 10% weight loss can significantly improve parameters. NHS ↗
🧪Environmental endocrine disruptors: Pesticides, BPA (plastics), heavy metals, solvents. Occupational exposure should be disclosed. ESHRE ↗

🔍 ABO System in Pregnancy

🩸ABO blood type determined at booking appointment bloods. Essential for cross-matching for any transfusion needs during delivery. NHS Blood Groups ↗
🧬Baby inherits one ABO allele from each parent. O is recessive — two O alleles needed. A and B are codominant. NHS ↗
⚠️ABO incompatibility (O mother, A/B baby): mild haemolytic disease of fetus/newborn (HDFN) may occur — usually mild, treated with phototherapy.
💉Blood group antibody screen performed at booking and 28 weeks. Detects unexpected antibodies that could cause severe HDFN. RCOG GTG 65 ↗
Rh Positive (Rh+)
D antigen present on red blood cell surface. ~85% of the population. No risks related to Rhesus factor in pregnancy for Rh+ mothers.
✅No Anti-D injection required during pregnancy
✅No risk of Rh sensitisation with any blood type baby
✅Standard pregnancy monitoring applies
Rh Negative (Rh–)
D antigen absent from red blood cells. ~15% of population. Requires specific management to prevent Rh sensitisation. RCOG GTG 22 ↗
⚠️Risk if baby is Rh+: maternal immune system can form Anti-D antibodies
⚠️Sensitisation in first pregnancy can affect all future pregnancies
⚠️Severe HDFN can cause hydrops fetalis, jaundice, anaemia, or intrauterine death
💉Anti-D immunoglobulin prevents sensitisation — highly effective

⚙️ HDFN Mechanism

Haemolytic Disease of the Fetus and Newborn (HDFN) — how it develops and why Anti-D works:
1️⃣Sensitising event: Fetal Rh+ red blood cells cross the placenta into Rh– mother's circulation (fetomaternal haemorrhage)
2️⃣Immune response: Mother produces IgM Anti-D antibodies (first sensitisation) — this pregnancy usually unaffected
3️⃣Second pregnancy: Mother produces IgG Anti-D rapidly — these cross the placenta and destroy fetal Rh+ red blood cells
4️⃣Anti-D prevention: Exogenous Anti-D given within 72 hours mops up any fetal cells before maternal immune system responds
5️⃣cfDNA fetal Rh typing (NIPD): Non-invasive prenatal diagnosis of fetal RhD status from maternal blood — avoids unnecessary Anti-D in Rh– babies. NICE DG25 ↗

💉 RAADP Anti-D Protocol — For Rh-Negative Mothers

⚕️ RCOG GTG 22 · NICE NG62 · NHS Guidance
📅Routine antenatal Anti-D (RAADP): 1,500 IU at 28 weeks (single dose) OR 500 IU at 28 and 34 weeks (two-dose). Both regimens equally effective. RCOG GTG 22 ↗
⏰72-hour rule: Anti-D MUST be given within 72 hours of any sensitising event or delivery. Earlier is better. Do not delay.
🏥After delivery: If baby is Rh+, mother receives postnatal Anti-D (minimum 500 IU). Kleihauer test quantifies fetal cells — larger doses if >4mL FMH.

⚠️ Sensitising Events Requiring Anti-D

🩺CVS (chorionic villus sampling) — 12–14 weeks
🩺Amniocentesis — any gestation
🚗Abdominal trauma (fall, car accident)
⚡External cephalic version (ECV)
🩸Antepartum haemorrhage (APH)
💊Medical or surgical termination
😢Miscarriage after 12 weeks
⚕️Ectopic pregnancy treatment
📚 RCOG GTG 22 — Anti-D Prophylaxis · NICE NG62 Antenatal Care · ACOG PB 181

Pregnancy Symptoms Guide

Trimester-by-trimester symptoms, management, dietary priorities, and warning signs. Sources: NHS · ACOG · RCOG

Common Symptoms
🤢Nausea & Vomiting (NVP): Affects 70–80% of women. Peak weeks 6–10. Caused by rising hCG and progesterone. Small frequent meals, ginger, B6 25mg TDS, sea-bands. Severe: see GP for Cyclizine. PSS ↗
😴Extreme Fatigue: Progesterone is a sedative. Rest without guilt. Iron levels checked at booking — supplement if low. NHS ↗
🍋Food Aversions & Cravings: Aversions may be protective against teratogens. Eat what you can tolerate. Cold foods often better tolerated in NVP.
🥈Metallic Taste (Dysgeusia): Hormonal — very common. Rinse mouth with bicarbonate of soda solution. Eat citrus, cold foods.
👃Heightened Smell (Hyperosmia): Linked to NVP severity. Avoid triggers. Fresh lemon scent may counter nausea.
🚽Frequent Urination: Begins early from hCG stimulating kidneys. Ensure adequate hydration despite urgency.
💤Breast Tenderness: Progesterone + oestrogen preparing ducts. Well-fitting supportive bra. Reduce caffeine.
💨Bloating & Constipation: Progesterone slows GI motility. Increase fibre, water, gentle walking. Lactulose safe in pregnancy. NHS ↗
😵Dizziness & Faintness: Blood pressure drops early pregnancy. Rise slowly. Stay cool. Small meals. If severe or with headache — seek care.
🔴Spotting: Implantation bleeding (light, pink, week 4) is common and normal. Any heavy bleeding requires same-day assessment. Tommy's ↗
😤Mood Swings: HCG and progesterone fluctuate dramatically. Normal. Inform partner. Rest and support are key interventions.
🌡️Hot Flushes & Night Sweats: Vasodilation from progesterone. Cool room (18–20°C), breathable sheets, cool shower.
📚 NHS Pregnancy Symptoms · Tommy's
T1 Dietary Priorities
🫘Folate 400mcg/day: Start before conception, continue 12 weeks. Neural tube closes by day 28 — most at risk before you know you're pregnant. High-risk: 5mg prescribed. CDC ↗
☀️Vitamin D 10mcg (400 IU)/day: Year-round in UK. Bone mineralisation, immune function. Free on NHS prescription in pregnancy. NHS ↗
🧂Iodine 200mcg/day: Critical for fetal thyroid and brain development. Iodised salt, dairy, fish. Supplement if vegan/vegetarian. EFSA ↗
🥩Iron 14.8mg/day: Red meat, lentils, fortified cereals. Blood volume increases 40–50% in pregnancy. Take with Vit C; avoid tea/coffee within 1hr. NIH ODS ↗
🐟Omega-3 DHA 200mg/day: Oily fish 2×/wk (not shark, swordfish, marlin). DHA for fetal brain. Algae supplement for vegans. EFSA ↗
🧀Avoid in T1: Liver & pâté (excess Vit A), soft unpasteurised cheeses (listeria), raw/undercooked meat, raw eggs, sushi, alcohol (no safe level). NHS Foods to Avoid ↗
☕Caffeine <200mg/day: 200mg = 2 espressos or 4 cups tea. Associated with reduced birth weight above 200mg. RCOG SIP 55 ↗
Second Trimester Symptoms
🏃Round Ligament Pain: Sharp stabbing pain in lower abdomen/groin, worse when standing quickly. Normal stretching. Warm compress, slow movements. NHS ↗
💗Quickening (First Movements): 18–22 weeks in first pregnancy. Earlier (16–18w) in subsequent. Like bubbles, flutters, taps. Tommy's ↗
😤Heartburn / GERD: Progesterone relaxes lower oesophageal sphincter. Smaller meals, avoid lying down after eating, head elevation, Gaviscon (safe). NHS ↗
🦵Leg Cramps: Nocturnal, calf cramps. Magnesium deficiency, dehydration, compression of blood vessels. Stretch before bed, magnesium 350mg, hydration. NHS ↗
🤧Nosebleeds & Nasal Congestion: Increased blood volume + mucous membrane swelling. Saline spray. Nosebleed: pinch soft part 10–15 minutes.
🌊Varicose Veins / Haemorrhoids: Increased uterine pressure on pelvic veins. Compression stockings, avoid prolonged standing, high-fibre diet. NHS ↗
🖐Carpal Tunnel Syndrome: Median nerve compression from fluid retention. Wrist splints at night, avoid repetitive tasks. NHS ↗
🌑Skin Changes (Linea Nigra, Chloasma): Melanocyte-stimulating hormone darkens midline, face. Normal and fades postnatally. SPF 50 reduces chloasma.
👁️Vivid Dreams: Increased REM sleep, hormonal changes. Normal. Avoid screens before sleep. Reassure partner!
🤸Back Pain (Low): Relaxin loosens ligaments + growing uterus shifts centre of gravity. Physiotherapy, pelvic support belt, gentle swimming. NHS ↗
📚 RCOG GTG 43 Pelvic Pain
T2 Dietary Priorities
🦷Calcium 1200mg/day: Fetal bone mineralisation accelerates. Dairy, fortified plant milk, sardines, kale, almonds. Deficiency → maternal bone loss. EFSA ↗
🥩Iron 27mg/day: Requirements nearly double. Haemoglobin checked at 28 weeks — supplement if below 105 g/L. NIH ODS ↗
🥛Protein 70–100g/day: Building fetal muscle, organs, placenta. Increases by 25g/day vs non-pregnant. Eggs, legumes, meat, dairy.
🥗Increase calories by ~340 kcal/day: Placenta, blood volume, fetal growth. Spread across nutrient-dense snacks. ACOG ↗
🐟DHA 300mg/day: Fetal brain growing rapidly. 2 portions oily fish/week + algae supplement if fish-free. Peak fetal brain growth now. EFSA ↗
💧Water 2.3L/day: Amniotic fluid maintained by hydration. Dehydration triggers Braxton Hicks. Urine should be pale yellow.
🌿Aspirin 150mg (ASPRE trial): If prescribed by physician for preeclampsia risk (e.g. 1st pregnancy, multiple risk factors). Start 12–16 weeks, continue to 36 weeks. ASPRE Trial — The Lancet ↗
Third Trimester Symptoms
😮‍💨Breathlessness: Uterus compresses diaphragm. Improves at "lightening" (~36w). Severe breathlessness at rest requires urgent assessment — can indicate PE. RCOG GTG 37 ↗
⚡Lightning Crotch: Sharp vaginal/pelvic zaps as baby descends onto nerves. Sudden, brief. Completely normal — baby engaging.
🏃‍♀️Braxton Hicks Contractions: Practice contractions. Irregular, short, no pattern. More frequent in T3. Drink water — often triggered by dehydration. Tommy's ↗
🏠Nesting Instinct: Sudden urge to clean, organise, prepare. Normal oxytocin-driven behaviour. Channel productively — don't overexert.
😴Insomnia: Discomfort, frequent urination, anxiety, restless legs. Left lateral position with pillow between knees. Avoid screens. NHS ↗
🦵Restless Leg Syndrome: Affects 26% of pregnant women. Iron deficiency is a major cause — check ferritin. Magnesium may help. NHS ↗
💧Oedema (Swelling): Fluid retention, especially ankles/feet in evening. Elevate legs, compression stockings, reduce sodium. Sudden facial swelling requires urgent assessment. NHS ↗
🔄Prodromal Labour: Irregular, painful contractions that stop and start. Normal in late T3. True labour: regular, intensifying, 5-1-1 rule. NHS ↗
🫘Rib Pain: Baby's limbs pushing against ribs. Sit tall, wear soft supportive bra. Side-lying improves comfort.
💦Colostrum Leaking: Yellowish first milk begins 16+ weeks. Normal. Breast pads, cotton bras.
📚 NHS T3 Symptoms · RCOG GTG 57 Fetal Movements
T3 Dietary Priorities
⚡Calories +450 kcal/day: Maximum fetal weight gain now. Focus on protein and healthy fats — avoid empty calories. ACOG ↗
🧠Choline 480mg/day: Critical for baby's hippocampus development in T3. Eggs (147mg/egg), beef liver, salmon, soybeans. 90% of pregnant women deficient. NIH ODS ↗
🫐Magnesium 350mg/day: Leg cramps, sleep, blood pressure regulation. Dark chocolate, avocado, legumes, pumpkin seeds. EFSA ↗
📅Dates (6/day from 36 wks): RCOG-reviewed RCT evidence: 6 dates/day from 36 weeks → cervical ripening, shorter latent phase, less need for induction. PubMed RCT ↗
🌸Raspberry Leaf Tea (from 37 wks): Traditional uterine tonic. Some evidence for shorter second stage. Do NOT use before 37 weeks. 1–2 cups/day. RCOG ↗
💊Continue: Vit D, Omega-3, Iron, Folate throughout all trimesters. Review with midwife at 36-week appointment.

🚨 CALL 999 / GO TO A&E IMMEDIATELY

🧠Seizures (eclampsia) — call 999 immediately
👁️Sudden visual loss, flashing lights, or severe visual disturbance
🩸Severe heavy vaginal bleeding (soaking a pad per hour or more)
💔Severe chest pain — possible pulmonary embolism (PE)
😵Loss of consciousness or severe collapse
🤱Signs of active labour before 37 weeks (preterm) — regular contractions before 37 weeks

⚠️ SAME-DAY ASSESSMENT (Call Maternity Triage / DAU)

🤕Severe persistent headache unresponsive to paracetamol + visual symptoms + swelling = possible preeclampsia. RCOG GTG 10A ↗
💧Sudden facial/hand swelling + headache — signs of severe preeclampsia
👶Reduced fetal movements from 28 weeks — do NOT wait, call triage. 10 movements in 2 hours is reassuring. RCOG GTG 57 ↗ · Count the Kicks ↗
💧Waters breaking before 37 weeks (PPROM) — go to hospital. Risk of cord prolapse and infection. RCOG GTG 73 ↗
🦵Unilateral calf pain/swelling/redness/warmth — possible DVT. Risk raised in pregnancy. RCOG GTG 37 ↗
🌡️Fever >38°C in pregnancy — see GP/triage same day. Listeria, UTI, Group A strep can all cause serious pregnancy complications.
🤮Inability to keep any fluids down for 24hrs — hyperemesis gravidarum, requires IV rehydration. RCOG GTG 69 ↗
🤕Right upper quadrant pain + jaundice — possible HELLP syndrome or obstetric cholestasis. RCOG GTG 43 ↗

Pre-Conception

Pre-Conception Planning

Optimise health before trying to conceive. Sources: CDC Preconception · ACOG · NHS

👩 Her Checklist

💊Start folic acid 400mcg (5mg if high risk) ideally 3 months before conception. CDC ↗
🩺Pre-conception GP visit: review all medications (many unsafe in pregnancy), chronic conditions, weight. ACOG ↗
💉Check rubella immunity. If not immune: MMR vaccination, then wait 1 month before trying.
🦷Dental check before pregnancy — gingivitis linked to preterm birth. Treatments safer pre-pregnancy. ACOG ↗
🚭Stop smoking completely — reduces conception time, miscarriage risk, and perinatal complications. NHS ↗
🍷Stop alcohol — no safe level in pregnancy. Consider stopping 3 months before TTC. NHS ↗
⚖️Aim for BMI 18.5–24.9 — being outside this range significantly reduces fertility and increases complications. NICE CG156 ↗

👨 Partner Checklist

💊Start Zinc 11mg + Selenium 100mcg + Folic acid 400mcg daily 3 months before TTC — sperm takes 74 days to mature. NIH ODS ↗
🩺Semen analysis if not done recently. Early screening prevents delayed diagnosis. WHO 2021 ↗
🚭Stop smoking — reduces sperm count 13–17%, increases DNA fragmentation 40%. Takes 3 months to see improvement. HFEA ↗
🌡️Avoid heat: no saunas, hot baths >10 min, tight underwear. Sperm production impaired above 34°C.
🍷Alcohol <14 units/week — above this level significantly impacts sperm quality.
🥗Mediterranean-style diet: vegetables, fish, nuts, olive oil — linked to better sperm DNA integrity. ESHRE ↗

🧬 Genetic Considerations

🩸Sickle cell carrier screening — offered routinely in UK. Especially important for African, Caribbean, Middle Eastern backgrounds. NHS ↗
🧬Thalassaemia screening — South Asian, Mediterranean, Middle Eastern backgrounds. NHS ↗
🔵Cystic fibrosis (CF) carrier testing — consider if family history or CF-prevalent backgrounds. HFEA ↗
📋Referral to genetic counselling if: >2 miscarriages, family history of chromosomal disorder, related partners. RCOG GTG 17 ↗

Nutrition

Regional Pregnancy Nutrition

Evidence-based foods by global region and trimester. All sources verified. WHO Nutrition in Pregnancy · FAO Food Composition · ACOG

🚫 Foods to Avoid in Pregnancy

🐟High-mercury fish — AVOID: Shark, swordfish, marlin, raw sushi tuna. Limit tuna to 4 cans/week. Mercury damages fetal nervous system. NHS ↗
🥚Raw/undercooked eggs: Salmonella risk. Only fully-cooked eggs. UK Lion mark eggs are safe at any doneness. NHS ↗
🧀Soft unpasteurised cheeses: Brie, Camembert, blue-veined unless cooked (listeria). Hard cheeses (Cheddar, Parmesan) safe. NHS ↗
🥩Undercooked meat & deli meats: Listeria, Toxoplasma. Cook all meat to steaming throughout. No cured/fermented meats unless cooked. FDA ↗
🍖Liver & pâté: Very high vitamin A — teratogenic in excess. Causes birth defects. Avoid all forms throughout pregnancy. NHS ↗
🌿Unpasteurised milk & juices: Listeria, E. coli, Salmonella risk. Always check pasteurisation label. FDA ↗
☕Caffeine >200mg/day: 2 espressos or 4 cups of tea. Above 200mg linked to reduced birth weight and miscarriage. RCOG SIP 55 ↗
🍷Alcohol — no safe level: Fetal Alcohol Spectrum Disorder. No safe level has been established at any gestation. Avoid completely. NHS ↗
🌱Herbal teas (many unsafe): Sage, pennyroyal, tansy, liquorice root stimulate uterus. Only peppermint, ginger, rooibos confirmed safe. NHS ↗
🦦Raw shellfish: Vibrio, norovirus, hepatitis A. Cooked shellfish is safe. Avoid raw oysters, mussels, clams. FDA ↗

Postnatal

Postnatal Guide

Sources: WHO IYCF · NHS Baby · NICE NG194

🌱 Development Timeline: Birth to 24 Months

All milestones are approximate ranges — children develop at their own pace. Discuss any concerns with your health visitor or paediatrician. Source: NHS Baby Development · WHO Growth Standards

🏥 Physical Recovery

🩸Lochia (postnatal bleeding): Normal for up to 6 weeks. Red → pink → yellow → clear. Foul smell, heavy clots, or fever → seek care. NHS ↗
💪Pelvic floor exercises: Start day 1 post-delivery, even if sore. 3 × 10 contractions/day. Continue 6+ months. Prevents long-term incontinence. POGP ↗
🪡Perineal care: Ice packs first 72 hours. Keep clean and dry. Stitches dissolve in 2–4 weeks. Warm bath (no bubble bath). NHS ↗
✂️C-section recovery: Avoid driving 6 weeks. No lifting above birth weight for 6 weeks. Scar massage from 8 weeks post-healing. NHS ↗
🦷Hair loss (Telogen effluvium): Peaks at 3–6 months. Normal — hormonal. Usually resolves by 12 months. Iron levels should be checked. NHS ↗
🩺6-week check: GP appointment for mum (and baby). Edinburgh Postnatal Depression Scale (EPDS) screening. Blood pressure, examination. NICE NG194 ↗
⚕️Physio referral: Diastasis recti, prolapse, perineal pain, scar pain — ask for Women's Health Physiotherapy. POGP ↗

⚠️ Postnatal Warning Signs

Seek urgent care:

🌡️Temperature >38°C — possible infection (endometritis, mastitis, wound infection)
🩸Heavy bleeding (soaking pad/hr) or large clots — possible retained placenta or haemorrhage
🤕Severe headache + visual symptoms — possible postpartum preeclampsia (can occur up to 6 weeks)
💔Chest pain, breathlessness, leg pain/swelling — possible PE or DVT (risk elevated for 6 weeks)
🧠Confusion, hallucinations, not sleeping: possible postpartum psychosis — call 999. APP ↗
📚 NICE NG194 Postnatal Care · RCOG

🤱 Breastfeeding

🌍WHO Recommendation: Exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months, then continue with complementary foods up to 2 years or beyond. WHO ↗
🥛Colostrum: First 2–5 days — yellow, concentrated, rich in antibodies (IgA) and growth factors. Perfect first food. Enough for newborn stomach size (5–7ml day 1).
📐Good latch signs: Wide mouth, chin touching breast, comfortable for mum, audible swallowing, no pain. Shallow latch is the main cause of pain. UNICEF Baby Friendly ↗
🕐Feed on demand: 8–12× per day in first weeks. Watch baby cues: rooting, sucking fists, turning head. Crying is a late hunger cue. La Leche League ↗
💊Support for common problems: Engorgement → frequent feeding + warm compress. Mastitis → continue feeding + antibiotics if fever. Cracked nipples → lanolin cream + check latch. NHS ↗
📞Support: National Breastfeeding Helpline: 0300 100 0212. La Leche League: 0345 120 2918. Local lactation consultant via GP referral.

🍼 Formula Feeding

✅All infant formulas meeting EU/UK standards are nutritionally adequate for the first year. No formula is superior to another. NHS ↗
🌡️Safe preparation: Boiled water at 70°C+ destroys pathogens. Do NOT use cold or room-temperature water. Cool before feeding. NHS ↗
♻️Never reheat or save leftover formula. Discard unused prepared formula within 2 hours at room temperature or 24 hours refrigerated. WHO Guide ↗
🧴Sterilise: Until 12 months. Electric steam steriliser, microwave steriliser, or cold water sterilising tablets.

🍠 Weaning (6 months)

🌍WHO & NHS: Introduce solids at around 6 months. Baby-led weaning (BLW) or traditional purée both valid approaches. WHO ↗ · NHS ↗
🥩First foods: Iron-rich foods critical (iron stores depleted by 6 months). Puréed meat, lentils, fortified cereals. Vegetables, fruit.
🥜Allergen introduction: Introduce peanut, egg, wheat before 12 months to reduce allergy risk. Not after. NHS ↗

💙 Postnatal Mental Health

😢Baby Blues: Days 3–5. Tearfulness, irritability, mood swings. Caused by hormone crash. Resolves within 2 weeks. Normal. RCPsych ↗
😔Postnatal Depression (PND): Affects 1 in 10 mothers and 1 in 10 fathers. Persists beyond baby blues. Low mood, loss of joy, negative thoughts, anxiety, difficulty bonding. TREATABLE. NHS PND ↗
⚠️PND vs Baby Blues: PND lasts beyond 2 weeks, interferes with daily functioning, includes thoughts of self-harm, and requires professional support. Tell your GP or health visitor. NICE NG194 ↗
🚨Postpartum Psychosis (rare, 1–2/1000): Sudden onset confusion, hallucinations, mania — psychiatric emergency. Call 999. Mother and baby psychiatric units specialise. APP Network ↗
😰Postnatal Anxiety: More common than PND. Intrusive thoughts about baby's safety, excessive checking, panic attacks. CBT and medication both effective. PANDA ↗
👨Partner mental health: 1 in 10 partners experience PND. Often overlooked. PANDAS helpline supports all parents: pandasfoundation.org.uk ↗
